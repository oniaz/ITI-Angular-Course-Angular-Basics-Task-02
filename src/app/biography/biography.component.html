Biography

Helen Beatrix Potter (1866–1943)

Born in London, Beatrix Potter spent her childhood in the company of animals and nature sketches. Largely self-educated, she developed a remarkable talent for scientific illustration — her fungi studies were presented to the Linnean Society of London.

At 35, she privately printed The Tale of Peter Rabbit after publishers rejected it. It was published in 1902 and became an immediate sensation, launching a beloved series of over 30 books.

With her royalties she bought Hill Top Farm in the Lake District, becoming a dedicated sheep farmer and conservationist. She donated over 4,000 acres of land to the National Trust upon her death in 1943.
